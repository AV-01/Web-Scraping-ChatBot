Future
 

 Notes 


‘Ir + a + verb’

 - In general, we use ‘Ir + a + infinitive verb’ phrases
    

when speaking of actions that will happen in the near future. We practiced this in Spanish 1 (Unit 5). 

 - In general, we use ‘Ir + a + infinitive verb’ phrases 
 
Examples: Yo voy a nadar. 

I am going to swim.

Ellos van a comer pollo. 

They are going to eat chicken..


Future Tense

 - However, we use the future tense when referring to actions in the more distant future.
 - Hint:
The clue word in English is "will_____" 
Examples: Yo nadaré el sábado.. I will swim on Saturday.

Ellos comerán pollo para La Navidad. They will eat chicken for Christmas.
Ellos comerán pollo para La Navidad. 
            　


Regular Future Tense Conjugations

 -

 Future tense does something we have not done before….
            For ‘-ar,’ ‘-er,’ and ‘-ir’ verbs, attach the following endings to the end of the infinitive:





é


emos




ás


-----




á


án







HINT: There is no stem changing, nor irregular yo in the future tense.

 
 YEAH!! 

Irregular Future Tense Conjugations

 -

 There are also some verbs that have an irregular
            future form. For these verbs, attach the same above endings to the following irregular stems:

Future Tense Conjugations that are also reflexive 

– 

You can also combine what you
            
learned before about reflexive verbs with the future tense. When you do this, you place the reflexive pronouns before the verb that you are putting in the future. 


Examples: Yo me ducharé. I will shower myself.
                 

 

Tú te cepillarás. You will brush yourself.

 

Nina se callará. Nina will quiet herself.
Nosotros nos peinaremos. We will comb ourselves.
